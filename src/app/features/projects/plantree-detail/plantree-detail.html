Plantree

A structured meal planning and pantry management product built around real-life household workflows.

Overview

Plantree is a meal planning and pantry management product designed around real household routines. It brings together planning, grocery lists, pantry awareness, and meal suggestions into one practical, connected experience.

Instead of treating these as separate tools, the product supports everyday decisions more naturally — from deciding what to cook, to organizing shopping, to using what is already available at home.

Plantree has been shaped through real household usage and continuous feedback, with a strong focus on clarity, flexibility, and real-life usability.

The home screen provides quick access to the main areas of the product, including planning, pantry, lists, and the meal library. Navigation is designed to be simple and consistent, allowing users to move easily between organizing meals, managing ingredients, and making decisions.

System

Plantree is built around a forward-flow system that connects planning, shopping, pantry management, and meal suggestions into one continuous cycle.

In practice, this flow connects everyday actions into a continuous cycle: planned meals generate grocery lists, purchased items are added to the pantry, and the pantry is then used to guide future meal decisions.

These transitions are designed to feel natural and lightweight, allowing users to move between planning, shopping, and cooking without friction.

Plantree also uses a shared user and space model. The same household users can move across spaces, while each active space defines what is current and relevant — especially for pantry-based decisions and shared activity.

Recipes remain reusable across spaces, while pantry data stays tied to the active space. This keeps the system flexible for different home contexts without duplicating core meal data.

The system follows a few key principles:

  • One-direction flow
    Data moves forward through the system, avoiding circular dependencies and keeping the logic predictable.
  • Independent features
    Planning, lists, pantry, and suggestions can work on their own, while still benefiting from being connected.
  • Real-time collaboration
    Shared grocery lists update instantly between household members, supporting coordinated shopping without conflicts or manual refresh.
  • Smart matching
    Meal suggestions are based on what is available in the pantry, prioritizing recipes that best match current ingredients while still allowing flexibility.
  • Real-life alignment
    The structure mirrors how households actually operate — planning ahead, shopping, and then deciding what to cook based on what is available.
  • Evolving over time
    As more meals are added and ingredient data becomes richer, the system becomes more useful, improving suggestions and decision-making naturally.

Planning

Planning in Plantree is designed as a flexible system that supports both structured weekly organization and more dynamic, date-based decisions.

While the week view provides a clear overview, users can navigate to specific dates and adjust plans at any time. Meals can be added, changed, or removed without disrupting the rest of the system, keeping planning lightweight and adaptable.

The home screen also provides a direct entry into the current day, opening the planning view focused on today's meals for quick access and adjustment.

Planning is also accessible through the meal library. From each saved meal, users can quickly add it to today, tomorrow, or any selected date, including multiple days at once.

Search is available both in the planning flow and within the meal library, allowing users to quickly find meals by name or ingredients. The matching is intentionally flexible, so even imperfect queries can still surface relevant results.

Date selection is handled within the meal library flow, where users can choose single or multiple days. In contrast, the planning view supports navigating across days while directly adding, removing, and adjusting meals for each date, including assigning responsibility where needed.

When adjusting a planned meal, users can replace it with another recipe from their meal library or create a new one based on existing data.

  • Weekly overview with day-level control
    Users can see their plan across the week while managing meals individually for each day.
  • Multiple entry points
    Meals can be planned directly from the calendar or added from the meal library.
  • Multi-day planning
    Meals can be assigned to one or multiple days, supporting both quick planning and repetition.
  • Lightweight interactions
    Adding or adjusting meals is designed to be fast and non-disruptive.
  • Supports real-life decisions
    Planning adapts to changes, rather than enforcing a rigid structure.
